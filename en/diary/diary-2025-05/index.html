Yam Bai Cha Plu Goong (05-30)

One of my two wild betel pepper plants had some leaves burnt by the sun. I cut these into strips this evening and made them into a southern Thai Yam Bai Cha Plu ( ยำใบชะพลู ) together with prawns, peanuts and coconut chips.

Gai Thod and Laap Hed (29.05.)

Tonight I had a spicy Thai “Laap Hed” ( ลาบเห็ด ) with chopped brown mushrooms and minced meat. I also had chicken drumsticks as “Gai Thod Nam Plaa” ( ไก่ทอดน้ำปลา ) from the Air Fryer.

Khao Soi Gai (05-28)

In the evening, I prepared a simple Northern Thai “Khao Soi Gai” ( ข้าวซอยไก่ ) with chicken breast and rice noodles. The curry was based on Yak Thai’s Khao Soi paste.

Khao Pad Ga Prau (05-28)

During my lunch break, I prepared a delicious “Khao Pad Ga Prau” ( ข้าวผัดกะเพรา ) with leftover rice, minced meat, and holy basil. A fried egg on top was a must, as this dish simply tastes better with it.

Gaeng Khiau Wan Gai (05-27)

For lunch today, I once again cooked a green Thai curry. This time with chicken, pea eggplants, and Bimi. The green curry paste from Yak Thai served as the base for this “Gaeng Khiau Wan Gai” ( แกงเขียวหวานไก่ ).

Nam Tok Hed (05-25)

Tonight, I prepared a spicy “Nam Tok Hed” ( น้ำตกเห็ด ) from northeastern Thailand’s Isaan region, following a recipe from Andy Ricker’s book “Pok Pok”.

Recommended Pok Pok at amazon.de¹

Sen Yai Rad Na (05-25)

For lunch today, I had wide rice noodles with thick sauce, or in Thai, “Sen Yai Rad Na” ( เส้นใหญ่ราดหน้า ). As for vegetables, I used the leftover Bimi.

Laap Moo Khua (05-24)

After a long hike in the black forest, I prepared a Northern Thai “Laap Moo Khua” ( ลาบหมูคั่ว ) for dinner.

Gunchiang Pad Khai (05-22)

For lunch today, I prepared the Gunchiang from the Chinese supermarket with eggs as Thai “Gunchiang Pad Khai” ( กุนเชียงผัดไข่ ).

Menu from Northern Thailand (05-21)

Tonight, I had three dishes from Northern Thailand: Nam Prik Ong ( น้ำพริกอ่อง ), Khao Soi Nam Naa ( ข้าวซอยน้ำหน้า ), and Tam Strobori ( ตำสตรอเบอร์รี่ ).

Khao Soi Yunnan (05-21)

For lunch today, I had a Northern Thai “Khao Soi Yunnan” ( ข้าวซอยยูนนาน ). This aromatic noodle soup is served with various side dishes like chicken, peanuts, pickled mustard greens, shallots, and lime. The recipe comes from Austin Bush’s book “The Food of Northern Thailand”.

Recommended The Food of Northern Thailand at amazon.de¹

Khai Kratha (05-20)

For dinner tonight, I made a quick Thai Khai Kratha ( ไข่กระทะ ) with fried eggs, tomatoes, Gunchiang, and Moo Yor.

Naem Khua Kap Khai (05-15)

Tonight, using the leftover fermented Naem sausage, I prepared a Northern Thai “Naem Khua Kap Khai” ( แหนมคั่วกับไข่ ) following a recipe from Austin Bush’s book “The Food of Northern Thailand”. This simple dish with egg and sausage is also known as “Naem Pad Khai” ( แหนมผัดไข่ ).

Recommended The Food of Northern Thailand at amazon.de¹

Pad Pak Dong Moo Sap (05-14)

During my lunch break today, I had Pad Pak Dong Moo Sap ( ผัดผักดองหมูสับ ) It’s a Northern Thai stir-fry with minced pork and pickled mustard greens.

Salad and Omelet (05-13)

For lunch today, I tried making a Thai “Khai Jiao Naem” ( ไข่เจียวแหนม ) with fermented sausage. I also had a “Yam Guai Tiao” ( ยำก๋วยเตี๋ยว ) with rice noodles and shrimp.

Pad Mama Gai (05-07)

Since I was in a rush for lunch today, I made a quick Thai “Pad Mama Gai” ( ผัดมาม่าไก่ ) with instant noodles and chicken. I got the idea for this simple dish from the book “Pok Pok Noodles” by Andy Ricker.

Recommended Pok Pok Noodles at amazon.de¹

A Child Of The Rice Fields (05-05)

Yay! My latest kitchen reading, “A Child Of The Rice Fields” by Ponpailin Kaewduangdee and Mick Shippen, arrived today

Thai Skewers (05-01)

On today’s public holiday, we woke our Japanese Konro grill from its winter slumber and kicked off grilling season. We had Thai “Gai Ping” ( ไก่ปิ้ง ) as well as “Nuea Sate” ( เนื้อสะเต๊ะ ). I prepared the beef skewers following a recipe from the book “Bangkok” by Leela Punyaratabandhu and served them with a sweet and sour chili sauce, as described in the book.
