Salad Kaek (03-31)

Tonight I prepared a Thai “Salad Kaek” ( สลัดแขก ). The recipe for this unusual salad with many different ingredients, dressed with a peanut dressing, comes from the book “The Best of Regional Thai Cuisine” by Chat Mingkwan.

Recommended The Best of Regional Thai Cuisine at amazon.de¹

Gaeng Plaa Ra Nor Mai (03-28)

Tonight I cooked a Thai “Gaeng Plaa Ra Nor Mai” ( แกงปลาร้าหน่อไม้ ). This unusual coconut milk curry is based on a paste made from lemongrass, garlic, shallots, galangal, kraichai and shrimp paste, and is seasoned with fermented fish sauce and dried fish. It includes pork and bamboo shoots as ingredients. The recipe comes once again from “Thailand: The Cookbook”.

Recommended Thailand: Das Kochbuch at amazon.de¹

Nam Prik Thua Fak Yao (03-27)

Tonight, I had another simple Northern Thai dish with yardlong beans: “Nam Prik Thua Fak Yao” ( น้ำพริกถั่วฝักยาว ). I served this spicy dip with grilled pork and pork cracklings.

Nuea Pad Nor Mai (03-26)

I made a quick Thai “Nuea Pad Nor Mai” ( เนื้อผัดหน่อไม้ ) for lunch today. The recipe for this stir-fried beef with bamboo shoots comes from the book “Classic Thai Stir Fry Dishes” by Hanuman Aspler.

Yam Woon Sen Hed Hom (03-23)

Tonight I had a Thai “Yam Woon Sen Hed Hom” ( ยำวุ้นเส้นเห็ดหอม ), a glass noodle salad with fried shiitake mushrooms and minced meat.

Beef and Salad (03-22)

After a long hike, tonight I had Thai “Nuea Pad Phed Krachai” ( เนื้อผัดเผ็ดกระชาย ) with beef, red curry paste, and fingerroot. Along with rice, I also had a “Yam Woon Sen Khaep Moo” ( ยำวุ้นเส้นแคบหมู ) with glass noodles, minced meat, and pork cracklings.

Wings and salad (03-21)

Tonight I made Thai “Pik Gai Thod” ( ปีกไก่ทอด ) again, following Ben Kindler’s recipe. I also had a “Yam Khai Tom Moo Sap” ( ยำไข่ต้มหมูสับ ) with hard-boiled eggs and minced pork, and a “Yam Plaa Muek” ( ยำปลาหมึก ) with octopus.

Recommended Bangkok Original Streetfood at amazon.de¹

Gaeng Som with Omelet (03-20)

During my lunch break today, I had a sour and spicy “Gaeng Som Gung” ( แกงส้มกุ้ง ) with shrimp and a wild garlic omelet. As a side dish, I had a mix of brown and white rice. I prepared the curry using Gaeng Som curry paste from Yak Thai.

Salad & Fried Chicken (03-18)

For dinner tonight, I prepared “Yam Naem Khao Thod” ( ยำแหนมข้าวทอด ) for the first time. This is a Thai dish with rice, fermented sausage, peanuts, and various herbs. I also had two chicken drumsticks from the air fryer on the side.

Recommended Bella Airfryer at amazon.de¹

Khao Khlong Pad Goong (03-17)

For lunch today, I made fried rice with shrimp and vegetables. For this dish, I used brown whole grain rice for a Thai recipe for the first time. The idea for this “Khao Khlong Pad Goong” ( ข้าวกล้องผัดกุ้ง ) comes from the book “Thailand: The Cookbook”, even though the dish is called “Khao Khlong Pad Samun Phrai” there. The brown rice gives this wok dish a whole new dimension and depth of flavor.

Recommended Thailand: Das Kochbuch at amazon.de¹

Curry and Sausage (03-16)

Tonight I had another dish with crab. This time the crab was an ingredient in a Southern Thai curry called “Gaeng Kati Poo Ma” ( แกงกะทิปูม้า ). I also had my unsuccessful “Sai Krok” (ไส้กรอก), but that’s a story for another time.

Two Seafood Dishes (03-15)

Tonight we had two Thai seafood dishes. First, steamed fish in soy sauce, and then a wok dish with crab and egg. For the “Plaa Nueng See Ew” ( ปลานึ่งซีอิ๊ว ), I used cod. The recipe for the “Poo Pad Pong Gari” ( ปูผัดผงกะหรี่ ) came from the book “Pok Pok” by Andy Ricker.

Recommended Pok Pok at amazon.de¹

Yam and Lon (03-15)

For lunch today, I used poultry sausage twice as a substitute for Thai “Moo Yor”. With it, I prepared a “Yam Moo Yor” ( ยำหมูยอ ) salad and a “Lon Moo Yor” ( หลนหมูยอ ) dip. The recipe for the dip comes from the Thai book “น้ำพริก เครื่องจิ้ม ยอดนิยม” (Popular Thai Dips) by Pimphitchaya Kokasivisut.

Lon and Laap (03-13)

Tonight I had a Northern Thai “Laap Moo Khua” ( ลาบหมูคั่ว ) with minced pork and “Prik Laap” spice mix. I also had a “Lon Naem” ( หลนแหนม ) with coconut milk and the fermented sausage “Naem”. The recipe for this unusual dip came from the Thai book “น้ำพริก เครื่องจิ้ม ยอดนิยม” (Popular Thai Dips) by Pimphitchaya Kokasivisut.

Or Lam Nuea (03-07)

For dinner tonight, I prepared an Or Lam Nuea ( เอาะหลามเนื้อ ) from Isaan. The recipe for this Thai beef stew with Laotian origins comes from the book ‘Hawker Fare’ by James Syhabout.

Recommended Hawker Fare at amazon.de¹

Pad Horapa Gai (03-07)

For lunch today, I used the leftover Thai basil from last night along with chicken to make a Thai “Pad Horapa Gai” ( ผัดโหระพาไก่ ). I followed a recipe from Rachel Cooks Thai.

Gaeng Phed Gai (03-06)

I spent the evening visiting our dear friend Sascha in Weil am Rhein. We decided to cook a red Thai curry with chicken together. It struck me how much I’ve been missing the experience of cooking with friends. For our “Gaeng Phed Gai” ( แกงเผ็ดไก่ ), we opted for the curry paste from Yak Thai.

Gaeng Moo Chamuang (03-04)

For lunch today, I prepared a Thai “Gaeng Moo Chamuang” ( แกงหมูชะมวง ), a dish featuring pork and Chamuang leaves – which I was finally able to find at the Asian supermarket. Those leaves have a unique nutty-sour flavor. The recipe was once again from “Thailand: The Cookbook”.

Recommended Thailand: The Cookbook at amazon.de¹

Ba Mee Rad Na Moo (03-04)

During my lunch break today, I made a Thai “Ba Mee Rad Na Moo” ( บะหมี่ราดหน้าหมู ) with pork and chestnut mushrooms served over Chinese egg noodles
