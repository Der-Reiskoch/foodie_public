Two Times Rice (02-28)

Tonight I took a culinary trip to Southern Thailand. First, I had “Khao Mok Gai” ( ข้าวหมกไก่ ), the Thai version of Biryani. And then, I also had “Khao Yum Ko Yo” ( ข้าวยำเกาะยอ ), a rice salad originating from the island of Ko Yo in Songkhla Lake.

Three Thai Dishes (02-27)

Tonight I had three Thai dishes. First, “Gai Thod Nam Plaa” ( ไก่ทอดน้ำปลา ), then a “Laap Moo” ( ลาบหมู ), and also a “Yam Guai Tiao” ( ยำก๋วยเตี๋ยว ) with poultry sausage.

Nam Ya Luk Chin Plaa (02-27)

For lunch today, I had Nam Ya Curry with rice noodles, which I haven’t made in a long time. If it had been made with the correct noodles, this curry with fish balls would be called Khanom Jeen Nam Ya Luk Chin Plaa ( ขนมจีนน้ำยาลูกชิ้นปลา ). To achieve the authentic Krachai flavor that characterizes this dish, I added some Silpin gastro oil.

Gai Pad Tao Djiau (02-26)

For lunch today, I made stir-fried chicken with yellow bean paste, mushrooms, and some vegetables. This “Gai Pad Tao Djiau” ( ไก่ผัดเต้าเจี้ยว ) is similar to the well-known “Rad Na” ( ราดหน้า ), but it is prepared with less sauce and served with rice instead of noodles.

Pad Kee Mao (02-21)

I seem to recall having a very honest and flavorful Thai “Pad Kee Mao” (ผัดขี้เมา) with chicken and rice as a side last night, after a few drinks at a cocktail bar.

Gaeng Phed Phik Gai (02-19)

For lunch today, I cooked a Thai “Gaeng Phed Pik Gai” ( แกงเผ็ดปีกไก่ ), a flavorful curry from the Isaan region, featuring chopped chicken wings and Thai eggplants. The recipe is from James Syhabout’s cookbook “Hawker Fare”.

Bok choy and ribs (02-16)

Since it tasted so good yesterday, today I prepared “Thai Si Khrong Mhu Dtun Tao Djiau” ( ซี่โครงหมูตุ๋นเต้าเจี้ยว ) again with spare ribs in an umami sauce. I served it with fried Bok choy and Prik Nam Plaa. For the ribs, I followed a recipe from the book Bangkok by Leela Punyaratabandhu and cut the ribs into smaller pieces before braising them.

Recommended Bangkok at amazon.de¹

Rad Na Gai (02-16)

I used the leftover sauce from the Thai braised pork ribs for a quick “Rad Na” ( ราดหน้า ) with chicken and bok choy for lunch today.

Salad and ribs (02-15)

Tonight’s dinner was a delightful contrast of Thai flavors: a rich and savory “Si Khrong Moo Toon Tao Jeaw” ( ซี่โครงหมูตุ๋นเต้าเจี้ยว ), a Thai braised pork ribs dish in a yellow bean paste sauce, and a spicy and refreshing “Laap Woon Sen” ( ลาบวุ้นเส้น ) salad with minced turkey and glass noodles.

Khao Tang Na Tang (02-14)

For dinner tonight, I prepared “Khao Tang Na Tang” ( ข้าวตังหน้าตั้ง ) with minced pork and peanuts. However, instead of the traditional rice crackers, I served it with crispy pork rinds and yard long beans.

Salad and curry (02-13)

Tonight I had a creamy Panang curry with chicken and a refreshing “Nam Tok Moo Yang” ( สเลิ้มจิ้นย่าง with grilled pork neck and fresh herbs.

Today, I cooked a super spicy Thai Pad Ka Prao Moo Sap ( ผัดกะเพราหมูสับ ) with udon noodles instead of rice for lunch. And there was also a fried egg.

Gai Thod Gratiam Prik Thai (02-11)
